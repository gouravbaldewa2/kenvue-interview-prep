Innovation

“How would you build an innovation pipeline for the hydration portfolio?”

+
  • Start with consumer insight mining — ethnographic research, social listening, HCP interviews
  • Map unmet needs across the hydration occasion spectrum
  • 3-Horizon Framework:
    • H1 (0-12 mo): Line extensions & format innovations on ORSL/eRZL
    • H2 (12-24 mo): Adjacent — children’s hydration, immunity combos
    • H3 (24-36 mo): Breakthrough — personalised hydration, gummies, dissolvable strips
  • Build stage-gate with go/no-go at each stage
  • Establish innovation council with R&D, supply chain, regulatory, commercial
Innovation

“Tell me about a time you identified a whitespace opportunity.”

+

Best option — Sprite Zero:

  • S: Gap in sparkling portfolio for health-conscious consumers
  • T: Lead product development and pilot to test viability
  • A: Cross-functional formulation work, 6-city pilot, measurement framework
  • R: 23% repeat purchase rate → national rollout

Alternative — Chai Point (strongest for 0→1): Built packaged foods from scratch. 4 sub-categories, INR 30M. No playbook, limited resources.

Innovation

“How would you launch a new SKU in the hydration category?”

+
  1. Consumer insight: What unmet need? Validated via quant + qual
  2. Technical feasibility: R&D formulation, FSSAI/regulatory compliance
  3. Commercial viability: Price-pack architecture, channel strategy, margin analysis
  4. Pilot: 2-3 test markets, success metrics (trial, repeat, velocity)
  5. Scale: National rollout playbook — supply chain, trade activation, media
  6. HCP strategy: Detailing/sampling with doctors and pharmacists
Innovation

“The electrolyte market is getting crowded. How would you defend and grow?”

+
  • Science moat: ORSL’s WHO compliance + Hydra-Activ Technology — lifestyle brands can’t replicate
  • HCP channel: Doctor recommendations as strongest purchase trigger
  • Occasion ownership: Own “illness recovery” (ORSL) and “smart daily hydration” (eRZL) distinctly
  • Innovation velocity: Stay ahead with formats, formulations, experiences
  • Price-pack: Rs 10 sachet to Rs 50 RTD — compete across the spectrum
Innovation

“What’s the difference between innovation in beverages vs. consumer healthcare?”

+
  • Regulatory: Health products need scientific substantiation; beverages are taste/format-driven
  • HCP influence: Doctors & pharmacists are a critical channel in healthcare
  • Trust: Efficacy-based > taste-preference. Science-backed claims matter
  • Speed vs. rigour: Beverages iterate faster; healthcare needs clinical validation

Her differentiator: “I’ve worked across the spectrum. Sprite Zero (taste-led). Merhaki (health-adjacent). Vedshakti (health claims). Rapid Hydration blueprint. My trajectory has moved steadily toward this intersection.”

“How would you build a culture of experimentation in a risk-averse org?”

+

  • Get senior sponsorship by showing ROI of experiments vs. traditional launches
  • “In a large organisation, the best way to drive change is to show results, not just make arguments.”

Behavioural

“You have 3 innovation concepts, limited budget. How do you prioritise?”

+
  • Define criteria: desirability (research), feasibility (R&D), viability (market/margin), strategic fit
  • Scoring matrix, but also trust consumer signal
  • Phase: pilot highest-conviction first, keep one in dev, park the third
  • “I’d rather kill two good ideas early than spread resources across three and execute none well.”
Behavioural

“What would you do in your first 90 days if the pipeline was weak?”

  • Days 30-60 (Diagnose): Map pipeline vs. 3-horizon. Consumer insight sprint.
  • Days 60-90 (Act): Refreshed roadmap to leadership. Clear bets + kill recommendations. Launch first pilot.

Killer

“How would you convince a skeptical R&D team to fast-track a concept?”

+
  • Show empathy for R&D’s constraints first
  • Bring compelling consumer data

Killer

“Zero successful launches in Year 1. What do you do?”

+
  • Diagnose first: consumer rejection? Technical failure? Commercial execution?
  • Own the outcome, diagnose honestly, rebuild
  • Reframe: “Year 1 might be about building the pipeline. I’d ensure robust pilots run so Year 2 delivers.”
Killer

“Science-led vs. consumer-led innovation — how do you think about this tension?”

+
  • “They’re not in tension — they’re in sequence.”
  • Start with consumer need, validate with science, bring to market with consumer-grade brand building
  • Example: Hydra-Activ in ORSL solves a scientific problem (osmolarity) that translates into a consumer benefit (faster rehydration)

Practice Tips

  • Record yourself on your phone and listen back — does it sound conversational or rehearsed?
  • If you go over 2:30, you’re losing them. Cut the weakest detail.
  • Make eye contact (or camera contact) during the closing paragraph — that’s where conviction matters most.
  • The bold numbers (8.9 share, 2.2X GP, 23% repeat, $1B, INR 30M) are your credibility anchors. Don’t rush past them.
  • Pause briefly before “What excites me about this role...” — the shift from past to future should feel intentional.
